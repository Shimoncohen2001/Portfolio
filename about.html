About Me

My name is Shimon Cohen. I’m a Computer Science graduate who has always been drawn to understanding how things work beneath the surface. More than the languages or the tools themselves, what interests me most is the structure behind a system — the logic that connects everything together. Over time, I naturally gravitated toward backend engineering and full-stack development, where I could work with architecture, data flow, and the foundations that make applications stable and reliable. I enjoy building things that are clear, organized, and meaningful, even when the problems are complex. I also have a growing passion for artificial intelligence. Not just as a trend or a tool, but as something that can bring new depth to software — a way to make applications smarter, more intuitive, and more helpful to the people who use them. I’m fascinated by how AI can be integrated into real systems, quietly improving the experience without taking the spotlight. At the end of the day, I’m simply someone who loves learning, solving problems, and constantly improving. I try to stay curious, patient, and open-minded in everything I build. And I hope to keep growing — both as a developer and as a person — one project, one challenge, and one line of code at a time.
